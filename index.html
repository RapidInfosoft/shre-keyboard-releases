A keyboard sees everything you type.
This one is built to forget.

Trust is the whole product in a keyboard. Here is exactly what Shre Keyboard does with your data — every statement below comes straight from the privacy policy.

  • No keystroke collection. The app does not collect, store, or share the text you type — and runs no usage analytics or telemetry.
  • No third-party trackers. Zero third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs. There is nothing in the app watching you for someone else.
  • The microphone is opt-in. Mic permission exists only for voice typing. Decline it and the keyboard still works — audio is not recorded or stored.
  • Encrypted in transit. When AI features do reach Nirlab servers, the connection is TLS-encrypted and no personally identifiable information is sent.
  • Works offline. On-device recognition keeps typing and dictation available with no connection at all. Your personal vocabulary is learned on-device and stays there.

One tap. Three engines.

Every dictation takes the fastest engine available and falls back gracefully — speed when you have it, coverage when you need it, and on-device recognition when you have nothing at all.

On-device

~0ms

Apple Speech and Google speech recognition, running on the phone itself. Instant partial results as you speak.

  • 50+ languages
  • Free, works offline
  • The default — most dictation never leaves this tier

GPU-accelerated

~50ms

NVIDIA Riva running on Nirlab's own self-hosted GPU infrastructure — not a rented black box.

  • 11 languages natively
  • Server-grade accuracy at conversational speed

Cloud fallback

~300ms

Whisper in the cloud, for the long tail — rare languages and hard audio the first two tiers can't cover.

  • 99+ languages
  • Used only when the faster tiers can't serve the request

Why a cascade? Most voice keyboards pick one engine and make you live with its trade-offs. Shre Keyboard routes each request — so everyday dictation is instant and free, and the expensive engines only run when they actually earn their latency.

On your phone in about two minutes.

Shre Keyboard is in private beta, so it installs as a direct APK from GitHub — no Play Store listing yet. Four steps, on the phone you're installing to.

  1. Download the APK

    Open this page on your Android phone (8.0 or newer) and tap the button. It goes to this project's GitHub Releases — the only official source for the APK.

    Download for Android

  2. Approve the install

    Android will ask once to let your browser install unknown apps — the standard prompt for any app installed outside Google Play. Allow it, then tap Install, and open the app once to finish setup.

  3. Enable the keyboard

    The path differs by manufacturer — these are the two most common:

    Stock Android — Pixel & most others

    Settings›System›Languages & input›On-screen keyboard

    Open Manage on-screen keyboards and switch on Shre Keyboard.

    Samsung Galaxy

    Settings›General management›Keyboard list and default

    Switch on Shre Keyboard — and set it as the default keyboard from the same screen.

  4. Switch and speak

    Tap any text field, use the keyboard-switcher icon (or long-press the globe key) and pick Shre Keyboard. Tap the mic and talk — the toolbar switches between Keys, Voice, and Tone.

Fair questions.

Why isn't it on Google Play?

Shre Keyboard is in private beta. While it is, we ship the APK directly from GitHub Releases so new builds reach testers the moment they're published, without store review lag.

Is installing an APK outside the Play Store safe?

Sideloading is only as safe as the source. The official APK is published in exactly one place — this repository's GitHub Releases, by Nirlab Inc. Download it only from there. The "install unknown apps" prompt you'll see is Android's normal check for any app installed outside Google Play, not a warning specific to this app.

Does it work offline?

Yes. Typing and on-device dictation work with no connection, and your personal vocabulary lives on the phone. AI cleanup, Smart Compose, translation, and Ask Shre need a network connection.

What happens to my voice audio?

Voice typing starts with on-device recognition, and audio is not recorded or stored. The privacy policy is the authoritative statement on data handling — it's short, and worth the two minutes.

Which devices does it support?

Any Android phone or tablet on Android 8.0 or newer. On iOS, the keyboard is in internal testing ahead of a TestFlight beta.
